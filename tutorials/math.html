College Algebra Tutorials

Introduction to College Algebra

College algebra builds on the algebra you learned in high school but with more emphasis on theory and applications. This tutorial will guide you through the key concepts you'll encounter in your freshman year.

Tip: Make sure you're comfortable with high school algebra before proceeding.

Linear Equations and Inequalities

Linear equations form the foundation of algebra. A linear equation in one variable can be written in the form:

\[ ax + b = 0 \]

Solving Linear Equations

Follow these steps to solve linear equations:

  1. Simplify both sides of the equation (remove parentheses, combine like terms)
  2. Use addition or subtraction to move variable terms to one side and constants to the other
  3. Divide both sides by the coefficient of the variable

Example 1:

Solve for \( x \): \( 3(x - 2) = 2x + 5 \)

Solution:

1. Expand: \( 3x - 6 = 2x + 5 \)

2. Subtract \( 2x \) from both sides: \( x - 6 = 5 \)

3. Add 6 to both sides: \( x = 11 \)

Quadratic Equations

A quadratic equation has the standard form:

\[ ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \]

Methods for Solving Quadratic Equations

  • Factoring: Express the quadratic as a product of two binomials
  • Quadratic Formula: \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)
  • Completing the Square: Rewrite the equation in perfect square form

Practice Problem:

Solve \( x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0 \) by factoring.

Solution:

1. Find two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to -5: -2 and -3

2. Factor: \( (x - 2)(x - 3) = 0 \)

3. Solutions: \( x = 2 \) or \( x = 3 \)
